About Me

I'm a first-year PhD student in Applied Mathematics at Cornell University. My research interests center around geometry, optimization, control theory, and robotics, with a focus on developing mathematical frameworks to solve complex real-world problems.

Before arriving at Cornell, I graduated from Creighton University in May 2025 with a BS in Mathematics (GPA 4.0). During my undergraduate studies, I was honored as a National Barry Goldwater Scholar and received the Creighton College of Arts & Sciences Dean’s Award for Distinguished Excellence. I've conducted research on optimal control at North Carolina State University's DRUMS REU program and signal processing as an engineering intern at MITRE Corporation.

Outside of mathematics and research, you can usually find me playing tennis, basketball, or pickleball, playing chess, reading fantasy novels, watching and reviewing movies, or writing and directing short films.
